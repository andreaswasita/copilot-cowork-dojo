# Escalation Playbook

When in doubt, escalate. The cost of an escalation is a 5-minute conversation. The cost of not escalating can be a regulatory finding.

## Escalation matrix

| Trigger | Route to | What to bring |
|---|---|---|
| Personal data about a named employee | HR / People Ops | What you have, who would see it, what you're trying to do |
| Customer-identifying or contract data | Legal | Document, audience, deadline |
| Pre-announcement financials | Finance + Legal | Source, audience, deadline |
| Security incident details | Security | Incident ref, what you know, who's been told |
| Anything you wouldn't post in a public Teams channel | Your manager | Plain English summary |
| Copilot produced something you suspect is biased / harmful / wrong-in-a-meaningful-way | Manager + responsible-AI lead | Prompt, output, why it concerns you |

## How to escalate well

1. **Pause.** Do not send, share, or save the artifact.
2. **Snapshot.** Capture the prompt, the output, and the source data.
3. **Frame the question.** Not "is this bad?" — "is this OK to send to <audience> for <decision>?"
4. **Default deny.** If the responder is unavailable and the deadline is tight, hold the artifact.

## Don't

- Escalate by forwarding the sensitive content over an uncleared channel.
- Escalate to "the group" when one named owner exists.
- Use escalation as procrastination. Set a return-time on every escalation.
